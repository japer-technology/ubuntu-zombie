# Voice & Tone

How Ubuntu Zombie sounds. This governs all copy in the promotion kit.

## Personality

Ubuntu Zombie speaks like a **calm, competent systems administrator who
respects that it is a guest on your machine.** It is precise, honest about
risk, and never breathless. The logo is the tone made visual: a listening
robot fused to a real PC, sharing one eye that belongs to the operator.

## Five principles

1. **Honest before impressive.** Lead with what the product actually does and
   what it deliberately does *not* do. We grant a root-capable identity; say so
   plainly. Trust is the differentiator, so never trade it for a punchier line.
2. **Operator-in-control, always.** Every claim should make clear the human
   owns the machine, the keys, and the kill switch. "You ask, it proposes, you
   approve, it acts, it logs" — that loop is the message.
3. **Concrete over abstract.** Show the command, the audit log line, the
   `127.0.0.1:7878` chat, the `doctor`/`repair` subcommands. Specifics beat
   adjectives.
4. **Calm, not hype.** No "revolutionary", "magical", "10x", or fear-based AI
   framing. The skull is weathered, not bloody; match that restraint.
5. **Plain language.** The product translates sysadmin work into plain English;
   the marketing should read the same way.

## Do / Don't

| Do say | Don't say |
| ------ | --------- |
| "A root-capable AI Systems Administrator you approve before it acts." | "An autonomous AI that runs your computer for you." |
| "Every action is audit-logged and reversible." | "Set it and forget it." |
| "You own the SSH key, the API key, and the kill switch." | "Trust us with your machine." |
| "Binds to 127.0.0.1; opt in to Tailscale for remote access." | "Access your PC from anywhere!" |
| "A transparent bash installer on a normal Ubuntu LTS system." | "A magic black box." |

## Words we use

audit log · approval · policy gate · operator · kill switch · reversible ·
transparent · local-only · key-only SSH · Tailscale (opt-in) · diagnose ·
explain · configure · repair · operate.

## Words we avoid

autonomous · takes over · unattended · set-and-forget · revolutionary · magic ·
unhackable · "AI replaces your IT guy".

## Tone by channel

- **Hacker News / Show HN:** maximally plain and technical. State the trust
  model up front, invite scrutiny of `SECURITY.md`, expect hard questions.
- **LinkedIn:** outcomes and responsibility; suited to operators and small-biz
  owners who maintain their own machines.
- **X/Twitter & Bluesky:** one sharp idea per post, a single screenshot, a
  link. Thread the trust model rather than cramming it in.
- **Reddit (r/linux, r/Ubuntu, r/selfhosted):** community-first, no marketing
  veneer, disclose that you are the author, link the repo and docs.
- **Press:** factual, quotable, with the boilerplate and the disclaimer.

## The one paragraph to memorise

> Ubuntu Zombie is a normal Ubuntu PC with an administrator inside it. Any local
> user can open a private chat, ask the machine to do something, see exactly
> what is proposed, approve it, and watch it happen — with every action
> audit-logged. SSH is key-only and root-disabled; remote access is opt-in over
> a private Tailscale tailnet. The operator owns the machine, the SSH key, the
> API key, and the kill switch.
